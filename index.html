I am an incoming Assistant Professor in the School of Computing at Southern Illinois University Carbondale (starting Fall 2025). Currently, I am completing my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Florida International University (expected Summer 2025), where my dissertation explores AI-driven defenses for cyber-physical and security of off-chain scalability solutions of blockchain. 

At SIUC, I will lead the Transformative Innovation for Trustworthy AI and Network Security (TITANS) Lab. My research interests span cybersecurity, machine learning, and distributed ledgers, with an emphasis on protecting autonomous unmanned aerial vehicles, smart grids, and off-chain Layer-2 blockchain protocols. I develop systematic defenses, intrusion-detection pipelines, formal verification workflows, and cryptographic protocol enhancements by combining data-driven modeling, program analysis, and zero-knowledge techniques. Looking ahead, my goal is to build resilient, self-healing security frameworks for next-generation autonomous systems and decentralized finance, ensuring that AI-enabled cyber-physical platforms remain trustworthy, adaptable, and secure by design.

Research Areas:

My primary research agenda centers on safeguarding next-generation autonomous and distributed systems: spanning cyber-physical platforms, edge IoT networks, and Layer-2 blockchains.
Specifically, I focus on:









I primarily apply data-centric machine learning, formal verification, zero-knowledge cryptography, and game-theoretic modeling in my research.

  • AI-driven intrusion detection for UAVs and other safety-critical CPSs
  • Security analysis and hardening of blockchain protocols
  • Trust, safety, and policy enforcement for reinforcement-learning-based autonomy
  • Privacy-preserving cryptographic protocols (e.g., zero-knowledge proofs) for cyber hardening
  • Resilient and secure resource orchestration across heterogeneous IoT infrastructures.
